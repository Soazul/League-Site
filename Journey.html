My Journey


Hey, my name is Annie. I have been an active League of Legends player since my sophomore year of high school. It all began when my parents brought me my first laptop; my brother suggested that I should play a game with him. That begins my journey with League and video games. I locked in Lux in the tutorial and learned the foundation of the game. I enjoyed playing Lux, so I became a Lux main for my first year. Afterward, I started to expand my champion pool in the support role. I started to play Zyra and Morgana, which I enjoyed because I had an easier time landing my skill shots. During the pandemic, I began to play more frequently with my friend. It was the main way my friends and I connected despite the distance. My friend was a Nami support main, so I explored my next main, Ahri. However, I quickly found out that last-hitting minions wasn't something I could do properly. As a result, I went back to the bottom lane and started to play Nami. Nami was an enchanter support, which didn't fit my usual playstyle of burst mages. Nonetheless, Nami is still a champion I actively play. After starting college, I had a small burnout with the game and took a short break and explored games such as Stardew Valley, For the King, Among Us, and Overcooked 2. I quickly got bored with those games as well and restarted to start my league journey. I wanted to try a new play style and champion, so I played a lot of ARAM. In my fourth game of ARAM, I played Rakan and I enjoyed the mobility in his kit. As a result, I started to play Rakan in my games, and I am now a Rakan main.


League of Legends and video games, in general, brought me joy and happiness on the days when I needed it the most. Playing video games with my friends brought me closer to them as we had a way to spend time together regardless of distance. In college, playing games allowed me to take a break from the stressful harsh reality and transported me to a world where I am stressed-free of real-world problems. I want to make games, to share this feeling with others. Therefore, I chose Computer Science and Game Development as my major.
