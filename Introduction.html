Introduction

Champion Classes

A champion's playstyle and primary role. Players may choose from 140+ champions.

  • Controller: Defensive champions who assist their ally by providing utility, such as shields crowd control. Subclasses: Enchanter and Catcher.
  • Fighter: Melee champions that both deal and sustain damage. Subclasses: Juggernaut and Diver.
  • Mage: Offensive casters that deal damage through their spells. Subclasses: Burst and Battlemage.
  • Marksman: Deal reliable damage at range.
  • Slayer: Agile damage-focused melee champions. Subclasses: Assassin and Skirmisher.
  • Tank: Melee champions who protect the team from incoming damage. Subclasses: Vanguard and Warden.

Runes

Personalize enhancements that add new abilities or buffs to a champion. Players may choose 2 rune paths.

  • Precision: Improve attacks and sustained damage.
  • Domination: Burst damage and target access.
  • Sorcery: Empowered abilities and resource manipulation
  • Resolve: Durability and crowd control
  • Inspiration: Creative tools and rule bending

Summoner Spells

Abilities that assist players. Players may choose 2 summoner spells.

  • Heal: Increase a champion amd a teammate's health, with a small movement boost.
  • Ghost: Increase a champion's movement speed and ignore unit collision.
  • Barrier: Shield a champion.
  • Exhaust: Slow and reduce the damage of a champion.
  • Mark (ARAM only): Throw a snowball at a champion and mark them.
  • Dash (ARAM only): Dash to the champion that is marked by the snowball.
  • Charity (ARAM only): Restore a champion and teammates' mana.
  • Flash: Champion blinks a short distance towards the cursor's direction. Hexflash: A channeling flash.
  • Teleport (Summoner Rift only): Champion blinks to an allied turret. Unreleased Teleport: Allow player to blink an allied turret, minion or ward.
  • Smite (Summoner Rift only): Deal damage to camp monsters, minions or dragons. Can be upgraded to Unleashed Smite and Primal Smite.
  • Cleanse: Remove all disables (aside from airborne, nearsight, and suppression) and summoner spells.
  • Ignite: Deal damage to enemy over time.

Maps

Howling Abyss Map: One lane with each team having 4 turrets, 1 inhibitor, 1 nexus and 1 portal with 2 portal destination.

Summoner Rift Map: Three lanes with each team having a jungle, 11 turrets, 3 inhibitors, and 1 nexus.

There are 5 roles: Top, Mid, ADC, Support, and Jungler, and 2 teams, red and blue. The objective is to be the first team to destroy the opposing team's nexus; players must destroy the outer turrets first, followed by inner turrets, inhibitor turrets, and nexus turrets. To accomplish this, players can kill minions and enemies or get objectives to earn gold and upgrade their items. Items increase a champion's abilities or defensive stat, and an optimal itemization helps a team close or increase the gap. When the game begins, Top and Middle go to their respective lanes, ADC and Support goes to the bottom lane, and the Jungler goes to the jungle. As the game progressed, the Jungler would ambush the enemy team with the help of their allies to get a gold lead. Around the 15 minutes mark, also known as the mid-game, players tend to focus on objectives like turrets, dragons, barons, and the Rift Heralds. Around the 30 minutes mark, also known as the late-game, players tend to focus on getting a lead by staying together and team fighting. Certain team compositions excel at different phases of the game, as there are early, mid, and late-game champions.

Game Modes

Standard: These game modes are always available.

Summoner Rift Map

  • Normal Blind Pick: Teams can't ban champions and can't see the opposing team's champions. There isn't a picking order, so both teams could play the same champion.
  • Normal Draft Pick: Teams can ban champions and see the opposing team's champions. There is a picking order, so each champion can only be picked once.
  • Ranked Solo/Duo: Competitive version of draft pick. Players can queue with 1 player around the same rank, unless they are Master+.
  • Ranked Flex: Competitive version of draft pick. Players can queue with 1, 2 or 4 other players, regardless of rank if the highest rank is Diamond. However, player must be at least Platinum to queue with Master+.
  • Co-op vs. Al: Players play against bots.
  • Custom Game: Players can choose their teammates and opponents.
  • Practice Tool: Players interact with training dummies.

Howling Abyss Map

  • ARAM: Teams are randomly assigned champions and players can reroll champions if available.

Featured: These game modes are made available on a rotation.

  • Legend of the Poro King: Win the assistance of the Poro King buff.
  • Nexus Blitz: A collection of minigames.
  • One For All: All champions on each team are the same.
  • Ultra Rapid Fire: Players have infinite mana.
  • Ultimate Spellbook: Players choose a random ultimate to replace a summoner spell.
